# Daily Growth System

> AgentsFactory's X/Twitter growth ops. Run this like a system, not a hobby.

---

## Morning Scan Pattern (30 min, 8:00-8:30 AM)

### Step 1: Check_notifications (5 min)
- Reply to anyone who engaged with your content overnight
- Prioritize: replies > quote tweets > retweets > likes
- If someone asked a question, answer it immediately

### Step 2: Feed_scan (15 min)
Open these in order:

1. **Following list** — scroll for 5 min, look for:
   - Pain-point tweets (manual work, hiring complaints, tool frustration)
   - AI/startup content with engagement under 50 likes (sweet spot)
   - Contrarian takes that are actually interesting

2. **Search: "automate" OR "AI agent" OR "no-code"** — look for:
   - Questions ("how do I automate X?")
   - Discussions with 10-100 replies (enough signal, not too crowded)
   - Tweets from accounts with 1K-50K followers

3. **Search: "manual" OR "repetitive" OR "spreadsheet"** — look for:
   - Frustration tweets ("I'm so tired of doing X manually")
   - Hiring tweets that are really automation problems in disguise
   - Founder pain tweets

### Step 3: Bookmarks (10 min)
- Bookmark 8-12 tweets worth replying to
- Tag mentally: value-add / pattern / story
- Pick the 3-5 best ones to reply to today

| **Daily total** | | **8-12 quality replies** |

### Reply Rules
- **Reply within 30 min** of original tweet for max visibility
- **Like first**, then reply (double notification)
- **Never reply to tweets with 500+ replies** — you'll be buried
- **Target tweets with 5-100 replies** — enough signal, room to be seen
- **One reply per person per day** max — don't be that guy
- **No threadjacking** — don't reply with your own thread
- **Match energy** — funny tweet = funny reply, serious = serious

## Content Mix Rules

### Weekly Content Split

| Type | % | Count/week |
|---|---|---|
| Replies (engagement) | 60% | 50-70 |
| Original tweets | 20% | 5-7 |
| Threads | 10% | 1-2 |
| Retweets + comment | 10% | 3-5 |

### Original Tweet Types (rotate these)
1. **Observation** — something you noticed from scanning signals/data (1-2/week)
2. **Micro-story** — a 2-3 tweet mini-thread from building AgentsFactory (1-2/week)
3. **Pattern call** — "the [X] pattern I see everywhere" (1/week)
4. **Build in public** — shipped something, here's what happened (1/week)
5. **Question** — genuine question that sparks discussion (1/week)

### Thread Rules
- 1 per week minimum, 2 max
- Always lead with a hook (story or counterintuitive claim)
- Never more than 8 tweets in a thread
- Each tweet must work standalone (people skip around)
- Last tweet = value, not CTA

### What NOT to Post
- ❌ Generic AI hype ("AI is transforming everything")
- ❌ Thread bois ("🧵 1/47")
- ❌ Hashtags (you're premium, act like it)
- ❌ "Just shipped X!" without context or story
- ❌ Quoting yourself
- ❌ Engagement bait ("What do you think?")
- ❌ Threads that are really blog posts
- ❌ Any post that could come from any AI startup (be specific)

## Weekly Targets

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Quality replies | 50-70 |
| New followers | 30-80 (ramp phase) |
| Profile visits | 500+ |
| Impressions on own content | 10K+ |
| Threads shipped | 1-2 |
| DMs initiated (inbound preferred) | 3-5 |
| Bookmark rate on threads | >5% |

### Ramp Phase (first 90 days)
The numbers above are conservative. Early on, focus 80% on replies and 20% on original content. You earn the right to broadcast by being valuable in other people's mentions first.

### Growth Phase (after 90 days)
Shift to 50% replies, 30% original, 20% threads. Your replies now bring followers because people recognize you.

## What NOT to Do

### 🚫 Common Mistakes

1. **Replying too fast with nothing to say** — speed without substance is spam
2. **Always pitching** — if every reply mentions your product, you're that guy
3. **Ignoring small accounts** — the 200-follower account today is the 20K account next year
4. **Copying competitors** — don't reply where they reply, don't match their tone
5. **Automation addiction** — never automate replies. Ever. The internet can tell.
6. **Consistency without quality** — 3 great replies > 15 mediocre ones
7. **Engaging only in AI bubbles** — go where the *customers* are, not other builders
8. **Treating likes as a metric** — likes are vanity. Replies to your replies and follows are what matter.
9. **Writing for quote tweets** — don't be controversial for reach. Be right instead.
10. **Forgetting to follow back** — if someone engages twice, follow them. It's the least you can do.

### 🚫 Anti-Patterns (death by cringe)

- Starting a reply with "Great point! 😊"
- Using "leverage" as a verb
- "As someone who works in AI..."
- Any variation of "you might find this interesting"
- Replying to a thread with an unrelated thread
- "This! 🔥" and nothing else
- Quote-tweeting someone to dunk on them
