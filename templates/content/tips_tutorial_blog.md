# 5 {topic} Lessons That Took Me Way Too Long to Learn

*Published {{date}} | 5 min read*

---

I've been building {topic} automations for {{years}} years now, and I've made every mistake in the book.

Here are the five things I wish someone had told me on day one.

## Lesson 1: Start With the Painful Task First

When I started with {topic}, I automated the easy stuff. The low-hanging fruit. The tasks that were simple but barely mattered.

Big mistake.

Six months in, the most painful part of the workflow was exactly as painful as day one. I'd built impressive automation that saved 15 minutes a week on something nobody cared about.

**Start where the pain is.** What task makes you groan? What's the one you'd pay someone to do for you? Automate that first.

## Lesson 2: Map Before You Build (Every Single Time)

I know you're excited about {topic}. I know you want to start building. I know sitting down with a whiteboard feels slow and boring compared to jumping into the tools.

Do it anyway.

The best hour I ever spent on {topic} was a whiteboard session where I discovered that 40% of our process steps existed only because someone once made a mistake in 2018 and never removed the workaround.

**Every hour of mapping saves four hours of rework.** This rule has never failed me.

## Lesson 3: Automate Decisions, Not Just Tasks

There's a difference between doing something faster and doing something smarter.

Speed is table stakes. The real power of {topic} is in decision automation:

- Instead of *"Send me the data and I'll decide"* → *"When X happens, automatically do Y"*
- Instead of *"I'll check in every morning"* → *"Alert me only when something needs attention"*
- Instead of *"Is this ready?"* → *"Validate automatically and route accordingly"*

When you automate decisions, you don't just save time — you eliminate bottlenecks entirely.

## Lesson 4: Test With Your Worst-Case Data First

Your {topic} automation will handle perfect input beautifully. Congratulations.

But then someone pastes a 4,000-row spreadsheet from 2019 into the system, and everything catches fire.

Test with the ugliest, weirdest, most broken input you can find. The edge case from the client who doesn't follow instructions. The file that has 14 columns when it should have 9.

If your automation survives the worst case, the rest is a breeze.

## Lesson 5: Measure Before and After

"You can't improve what you can't measure" is a cliché because it's true.

Before starting any {topic} project, document the current state:
- **Time:** How many person-hours per week?
- **Errors:** What's the failure/rework rate?
- **Cost:** What does this actually cost in money and morale?

Without a baseline, you can't prove the value of what you've built. And you definitely can't iterate on it effectively.

## The Meta-Lesson

All five of these lessons share a common theme: **the slow, disciplined work is the fast work.** Mapping, measuring, testing with bad data, and starting with the hard stuff — these feel like delays. They're actually accelerators.

The fastest path to great {topic} automation is slowing down at the start.
