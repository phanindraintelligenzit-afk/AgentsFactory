I get asked a lot: "What's it actually like to build {topic} automation?"

The honest answer? It's less Hollywood hacker montage, more detective story.

## Phase 1: The Crime Scene (Discovery)

Every automation engagement starts with me being nosy. Aggressively nosy.

"Tell me about a normal Tuesday."
"Okay, now show me exactly what happens at 2:17pm."
"Wait — you copy that into *where* now?"

The discovery phase isn't glamorous. It's sitting with the people who do the work, watching them click, copy, paste, cross-reference, and pray nothing breaks. But it matters more than everything that follows. 

**Because automating a broken process just gives you faster broken results.**

### What We Find (Every Time):
- Steps that exist because "that's how we've always done it"
- Approval chains that exist because someone was burned once (in 2019)
- Information that gets re-typed 3 times across 3 systems

## Phase 2: The Blueprint (Process Mapping)

This is where the mess becomes a map. Every decision point, every handoff, every "it depends" gets documented.

A good process map is disruptive — it makes invisible work visible, and that first draft always triggers the same reaction:

*"I didn't realize we were doing all that.}"*

A great process map separates the essential from the accidental. The essential stays, gets streamlined, and gets automated. The accidental? Gets deleted.

## Phase 3: The Build

Phase 3 is where I get to play. But "play" is generous. It's more like:

- Day 1-2: Core automation wiring. Basic flow works. Feeling invincible.
- Day 3-4: Edge cases emerge. Feeling humble.
- Day 5: The 11pm breakthrough that makes the whole thing sing.
- Day 6-7: Testing, breaking, fixing, testing again.

### The Build Truths That Nobody Tweets:
1. The first working version does 60% of what's needed
2. The remaining 40% takes longer than the first 60%
3. The best feature is always discovered accidentally
4. Testing should take as long as building (it never gets that time)

## Phase 4: The Handoff (Hardest Part)

Here's the dirty secret of the automation industry: **the technology is the easy part.** The hard part is trust.

Your team has been doing {topic} a certain way for years. They're good at it. And now you're asking them to trust a machine.

Our solution: Shadow mode. The automation runs invisibly alongside the existing process for two weeks. Outputs are compared. Nobody's workflow is disrupted. And when the automation proves itself? The switch is easy.

## The Bottom Line

{topic} automation isn't magic. It's methodical. It's discovery, design, build, test, and change management — in that order.

If someone tells you it's quick and painless, they're selling you a fairy tale.
