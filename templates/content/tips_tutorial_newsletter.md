## 1. Start With the Painful Task First

Not the easiest. Not the most obvious. The one you dread.

If {topic} automation doesn't eliminate the thing you hate most, you're solving the wrong problem. Start where the pain is and work backward.

## 2. Map Before You Build (Always)

Every hour of mapping saves four hours of rework.

Grab a whiteboard or a piece of paper. Write out every single step of your {topic} process. Include the ones you do without thinking.

You'll find that roughly 30% of your steps exist out of habit, not necessity. Delete them before you automate.

## 3. Automate Decisions, Not Just Tasks

Doing things faster is table stakes. The real power of {topic} is making the right decision without human intervention.

Instead of: *"Send me the data and I'll decide what to do"*
Build: *"When X happens, automatically do Y."*

That's automation with a brain.

## 4. Test With Your Worst-Case Data First

Your {topic} automation will work beautifully with clean, perfect data. That's not the test.

Feed it the messiest input you've ever seen. The weird edge case from March 2023. The time someone typed everything in ALL CAPS.

If it handles the ugliest case, the rest is easy.

## 5. Measure Before and After

You can't improve what you can't measure.

Before you start any {topic} project, ask:
- How much time does this take now?
- What's the error rate?
- What does it cost (in money and sanity)?

If you can't answer these questions, start there. The answers will define everything that follows.
