## Building the Automation

We didn't throw technology at the problem. We started with the process:

### Step 1: Map Everything
Every decision, every handoff, every "I'll just check this real quick." Two hours with a whiteboard revealed the real workflow — which looked nothing like the documented one.

### Step 2: Identify the Patterns
80% of the work followed identical patterns. This was the automation goldmine.

### Step 3: Build the Pipeline
- **Smart intake forms** with self-validation and auto-categorization
- **Conditional routing** based on workload type, complexity, and available capacity
- **Cross-tool sync** that eliminated double-entry entirely
- **Proactive error detection** that catches issues before clients notice

### Step 4: Test, Tune, Transfer
Three weeks of parallel running. Two weeks of tuning. One knowledge transfer session. Done.
