## Week 1: Discovery (a.k.a. "Shock and Awe")

Every project starts the same way. The client describes their workflow casually. And we start asking questions. Really specific questions. 

"Wait — you're doing that *manually*?"

"Yeah, it takes about 3 minutes."

"Okay, and how many times a day?"

"...About 40. But it's fine."

It's not fine.

**Discovery deliverable:** A process map that usually makes the client uncomfortable. In a good way.

## Week 2: Design & Build

This is where the magic happens. And by "magic" I mean staring at error logs at 11pm.

The honest truth about building {topic} automation:

- The first version always does ~60% of what you want
- The remaining 40% takes twice as long as the first 60%
- Edge cases are discovered by breaking things on purpose
- The best feature is always one the client didn't ask for (but desperately needed)

## Week 3: The Human Factor

Here's what nobody tells you: **the technology is the easy part.**

The hard part is getting a team that's done something the same way for 5 years to trust a new system.

Our approach: shadow mode first. The automation runs alongside existing processes for 2 weeks. Output is compared. Confidence is built. *Then* we flip the switch.
