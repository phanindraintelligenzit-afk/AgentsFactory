## WORKFLOW 1: Content Week Engine
**Purpose:** Turn one YouTube idea into a full week of content using a multi-agent team.

**When to use:** Weekly content planning, showing clients the power of multi-agent workflows.

**Prompt:**
```
Hermes, I want to turn one YouTube video idea into a full week of content for my business.

The idea is:
"How AI Agents Can Automate 80% of a Small Business's Repetitive Tasks"

Create a small AI team workflow for this.

I want each role to have one clear job:
1. Research the topic with real examples and data
2. Find the strongest hook/angle for the video
3. Create a detailed YouTube video outline (10-15 min video)
4. Turn it into a newsletter draft for email subscribers
5. Turn it into 3 LinkedIn posts (different angles)
6. Review everything for clarity, usefulness, and natural voice

For each role, tell me:
- What the role is responsible for
- What it should produce
- What order the work should happen in

Then give me:
1. The YouTube video outline with timestamps
2. A newsletter draft based on the idea
3. 3 LinkedIn post drafts (hook, body, CTA)
4. 5 short-form video ideas (Reels/Shorts/TikToks)
5. A short review of what I should change before publishing

Keep it practical, plain language, and professional. Do not make it hypey. Do not call this an AI employee team or autonomous business. Frame it as a few agents, each with one clear job.
```

**Expected output:** YouTube outline, newsletter, 3 LinkedIn posts, 5 short-form ideas, review notes.

## WORKFLOW 2: Offer Builder
**Purpose:** Analyze competitor offers and get recommendations for your own service packages.

**When to use:** Strategic planning, pricing, creating service tiers.

**Prompt:**
```
I'm considering creating different service packages for AgentsFactory, my AI automation agency.

I'm giving you two URLs of two very different AI service businesses — one premium, one budget. I want you to analyze both offers, compare them to what I currently do, and give me a recommendation on what packages I should create.

Make sure you ask me any questions you're not clear on prior to doing this.

[URL OF PROGRAM 1 — premium AI consulting firm]
[URL OF PROGRAM 2 — budget AI automation service]

Here's what my current setup is:
AgentsFactory provides AI agent workflow automation for small and medium businesses. We use Hermes Agent (Nous Research), Claude/OpenRouter, Telegram/Slack integration. We automate repetitive tasks like customer support, lead follow-up, content scheduling, data entry, and reporting. Currently solo operator (Phani), targeting businesses with 10-50 employees who spend 10+ hours/week on repetitive tasks.

Recommend:
1. Three service tiers (starter, growth, enterprise)
2. Pricing for each tier
3. What's included in each
4. What makes someone upgrade from one tier to the next
```

**Expected output:** 3 service tiers with pricing, positioning, and upgrade incentives.

## WORKFLOW 3: Client Onboarding Automator
**Purpose:** Create a standardized, automated client onboarding workflow.

**When to use:** Setting up operations for AgentsFactory client delivery.

**Prompt:**
```
I need to build a standardized client onboarding workflow for AgentsFactory.

When a new client signs up, these things need to happen automatically:
1. Send a welcome email with next steps
2. Create a dedicated Slack channel for the client workspace
3. Set up a project board (Notion or GitHub Projects)
4. Generate a client intake form (Google Form or Typeform)
5. Schedule a kickoff call (send Calendly link)
6. Create a shared folder in Google Drive/Dropbox
7. Set up monitoring for the automations we build for them

Help me turn this into a simple project plan with:
1. The master checklist
2. Tools for each step
3. What's manual vs what can be automated
4. How to measure if onboarding went smoothly (time to first value, client satisfaction score)
5. A checklist template I can reuse for every new client

Keep it practical. No hype. The goal is to make onboarding feel premium but require minimal manual work from me.
```

**Expected output:** Project plan, master checklist, tool recommendations, measurement criteria.

## WORKFLOW 5: Business Command Center
**Purpose:** Build a dashboard to run AgentsFactory from one place.

**When to use:** Operations setup, getting visibility into your business.

**Prompt:**
```
I want to build a command center for AgentsFactory — my AI automation agency.

Here's what I want to see at a glance:
1. Active client projects and their status
2. Revenue this month vs target
3. Automated workflows running and their health
4. Lead pipeline (potential clients in funnel)
5. Content calendar (what's being published when)
6. Key metrics: hours saved for clients, tasks automated, uptime %

Look at this layout and recreate something similar for AgentsFactory. Before you build, ask me:
- What tools I currently use (Notion, Google Sheets, Slack, Telegram)
- Where this should live (Notion page, web dashboard, etc.)
- How often I'll check it (daily, weekly)

Then build it.
```

**Expected output:** A functional command center/dashboard tailored to AgentsFactory.

## WORKFLOW 6: Market Research Engine
**Purpose:** Run a market research refresh on the AI automation space.

**When to use:** Monthly/quarterly market intelligence, content planning, sales preparation.

**Prompt:**
```
Have Researcher run a market research refresh for AgentsFactory.

Focus: AI agent workflow automation for small and medium businesses — trends, competitors, pricing, tools, and opportunities in 2025-2026.

Use my existing business context and write the outputs to:
C:\Users\Admin\Projects\AgentsFactory\docs\research\

Separate findings into:
1. Observed evidence (data, numbers, verifiable facts)
2. Claims (what companies say about themselves)
3. Verified knowledge (cross-referenced facts)
4. Weak signals (early trends that could become big)
5. Content opportunities (topics I should write/speak about)
6. Sales intelligence (what prospects care about, objections, language they use)

Then send me the operator brief here — a 1-page summary of what matters most for my business right now.
```

**Expected output:** Research brief with evidence, claims, signals, content ideas, and sales intelligence.

Turn this into a LinkedIn post for my audience. Use my voice, AgentsFactory positioning, and include a hook, body, and CTA.

```
Also give me:
1. 3 headline options
2. A shorter version for Twitter/X
3. A newsletter blurb version
```

**Prompt (Newsletter version):**
```
I just had an idea for this week's newsletter. The point is that [YOUR IDEA HERE]. Turn this into a draft for my audience. Use my voice, AgentsFactory positioning, and include 3 subject lines, preview text, and the final draft.
```

**Expected output:** Finished content drafts ready to publish.

## Tips
1. **Give Hermes context first** — the more it knows about AgentsFactory, the better every answer
2. **Let it ask questions** — when a prompt says "ask me anything you're not clear on," actually answer
3. **Keep it in your voice** — tell Hermes to drop the hype and write like you talk
4. **Start small** — run one prompt, see what comes back, then tweak for your workflow
